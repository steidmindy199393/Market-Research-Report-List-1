<p><p>The Indium Gallium Arsenide (InGaAs) camera market is categorized into cooled and uncooled technology. Cooled InGaAs cameras utilize thermoelectric cooling systems to reduce thermal noise, enhancing sensitivity and imaging quality, especially in low-light environments. In contrast, uncooled InGaAs cameras operate at ambient temperatures, offering a more compact and cost-effective solution, though with lower sensitivity compared to their cooled counterparts. Each type serves distinct applications, such as scientific research, surveillance, and industrial inspections, addressing various user needs in infrared imaging.</p></p>
