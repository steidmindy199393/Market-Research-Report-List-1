<p><p>The dry beer market primarily encompasses two types: pasteurized beer and draft beer. Pasteurized beer undergoes a heat treatment process that extends its shelf life by eliminating harmful microorganisms, making it ideal for mass distribution. On the other hand, draft beer is served directly from kegs, emphasizing freshness and flavor, often found in bars and restaurants. Both types cater to diverse consumer preferences, with pasteurized beer focusing on longevity and widespread availability, while draft beer highlights quality and immediate consumption experience.</p></p>
