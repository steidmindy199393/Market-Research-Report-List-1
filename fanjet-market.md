<p><p>The fanjet market is divided into high-pressure and low-pressure turbine segments, each serving distinct roles in aviation propulsion. High-pressure turbines operate at elevated temperatures and pressures, enhancing efficiency and performance in commercial and military jets. They are critical in extracting energy from combustion gases. In contrast, low-pressure turbines are designed to handle larger volumes of air at lower pressures, focusing on thrust generation and optimizing fuel consumption. Both types are essential for balancing performance, efficiency, and environmental standards in modern aircraft.</p></p>
