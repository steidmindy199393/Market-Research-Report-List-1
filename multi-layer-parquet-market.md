<p><ul><li>Glued</li><li>Floating</li></ul></p>
<p><p>In the multi-layer Parquet market, two primary types exist: glued and floating. Glued parquet features layers bonded together using adhesive, ensuring stability and permanence, making it ideal for high-traffic areas. Floating parquet, on the other hand, is installed without gluing, allowing for easier replacement and maintenance. This method relies on interlocking planks to create a stable surface while enabling slight movement. Both types cater to different preferences in aesthetics, installation ease, and flooring durability.</p></p>
