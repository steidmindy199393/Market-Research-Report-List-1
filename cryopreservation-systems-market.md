<p><p>The cryopreservation systems market includes various types of storage solutions essential for preserving biological materials. Blood refrigerators maintain temperatures between 4℃ and ±1℃ to store blood products safely. Medical refrigerators operate at 2℃ to 8℃, ideal for vaccines and pharmaceuticals. Biomedical freezers function within -10℃ to -50℃ for tissue samples, while ultra-low freezers (-50℃ to -150℃) are vital for long-term storage of cells and enzymes. Liquid nitrogen tanks (-150℃ to -196℃) are used for cryogenic preservation. Other market types encompass specialized storage devices catering to diverse biomedical applications.</p></p>
