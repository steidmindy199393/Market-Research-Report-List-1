<p><p>The shoe orthotics market primarily consists of two types: rigid orthotics and soft orthotics. Rigid orthotics are made from firm materials, providing strong support and alignment for conditions like flat feet or high arches. They are often used in corrective footwear to stabilize the foot. In contrast, soft orthotics are crafted from cushioned materials, offering comfort and shock absorption, making them suitable for conditions such as plantar fasciitis. Each type addresses different foot needs, enhancing overall foot health and mobility.</p></p>
